Exploring a shared visual language for mixed-ability co-creation

Semio is an early research prototype exploring how visual symbols and AI can support mixed-ability co-creation. It is intentionally incomplete - designed as a starting point for participatory critique rather than a finished tool. The aim is to invite feedback from people with intellectual disabilities and their support networks, so the direction can be shaped by their input. Here, AI acts as a transparent interpreter to help confirm meaning, not as a voice that speaks for anyone.

How the demo works
1

Compose with symbols

Drag shapes, colours, objects and emotions onto a shared canvas.

2

Review the AI’s interpretation

The system proposes a possible meaning, plus alternative readings.

3

Confirm or correct

Tap one of the suggested readings to confirm or refine the interpretation.

This is an early demonstration prototype developed as a starting point for participatory co-design. It is intentionally incomplete. The readings shown are pre-scripted for illustration only - there is no real AI model running. Everything operates in-browser with no data collected. The purpose of this prototype is to surface assumptions and invite critique from people with intellectual disabilities and their support networks. Best viewed on desktop.
